# Global Word-Building Strategies: Quick Reference
## For LBOS Contributors and Moderators

This is a quick-reference card for anyone coining new Sesotho STEM words. 
Before creating a loanword, study how other languages solved the same problem.

---

## The Golden Rule

> **Translate the MEANING, not the SOUND.**
> 
> Ask yourself: "What does this thing DO?" — then express that in Sesotho.

---

## Strategy 1: Semantic Calque (🏆 Best)

**Inspired by**: Chinese 🇨🇳, Hebrew 🇮🇱

**How**: Break the English concept into "what it DOES" and express that using Sesotho roots.

**Steps**:
1. What does the thing DO? (function)
2. What is it LIKE? (metaphor)
3. What is its ESSENCE? (core meaning)
4. Find Sesotho verbs/nouns that describe those functions
5. Combine them with appropriate class prefixes

**Examples from Chinese**:
- Computer = 电脑 = "electric brain" → Sesotho: kelellomotlakase
- Engine = 发动机 = "emit-motion-machine" → Sesotho: setsamaisi
- Microscope = 显微镜 = "reveal-tiny-mirror" → Sesotho: sepontshenyanane

---

## Strategy 2: Compounding (🔗 Excellent)

**Inspired by**: German 🇩🇪, Icelandic 🇮🇸

**How**: Join two or more existing Sesotho words into one new word.

**Pattern**: Modifier + Head noun

**Examples from German**:
- Airplane = Flugzeug = "flight-thing" → Sesotho: sefofane (from ho fofela)
- Vocabulary = Wortschatz = "word-treasure" → Sesotho: letlotsomantswe

**The Icelandic gold standard**:
- Computer = tölva = "number-prophetess" (poetic!)
- Telephone = sími = "long thread" (beautiful!)

---

## Strategy 3: Nominalization (🔧 Good)

**Inspired by**: Bantu languages 🌍, Arabic 🇸🇦

**How**: Derive a noun from a Sesotho verb using class prefixes and suffixes.

**Most useful prefix**: **se-** (Class 7 = instrument/tool)

**Formula**: se- + verb stem + -i/-o

| Verb | Nominalization | Meaning |
|:---|:---|:---|
| ho bala (count) | se-bal-i | counting tool (computer) |
| ho tsamaisa (drive) | se-tsamais-i | driving tool (engine) |
| ho bontsha (show) | se-bontsh-i | showing tool (display) |
| ho lekanya (measure) | se-lekany-i | measuring tool |

---

## Strategy 4: Semantic Extension (🔄 Acceptable)

**Inspired by**: Japanese 🇯🇵, Icelandic 🇮🇸

**How**: Take an existing Sesotho word and give it a new technical meaning.

**Example**: Icelandic "sími" was an ancient word for "long thread" — repurposed to mean "telephone."

**Sesotho possibilities**:
- "seipone" (mirror) could extend to mean "lens" or "screen"
- "tsela" (path/road) could extend to mean "circuit" or "network"

---

## Strategy 5: Loanword (⚠️ Last Resort)

**When to use**: Only when strategies 1-4 genuinely cannot produce a workable word.

**If you must**: At least adapt the phonology to Sesotho rules:
- Must end in a vowel
- Must use CV (consonant-vowel) syllable structure
- Replace: v→b, z→s, x→ks, c→k, g→k, d→t
